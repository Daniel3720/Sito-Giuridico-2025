Frequently Asked Legal Questions (FAQ)

What is an ordinary law?

It is a normative act adopted by Parliament, with a legislative procedure, and is hierarchically below the Constitution and constitutional laws.

What does the principle of legality mean?

No one can be punished except by virtue of a law that was in force at the time the act was committed (Art. 25(2) of the Constitution; Art. 1 Criminal Code).

What is the difference between a law and a legislative decree?

A law is approved directly by Parliament. A legislative decree is issued by the Government under parliamentary delegation (Art. 76 Const.).

What is the Official Gazette?

It is the official journal for the publication of laws, decrees and judgments. Without publication, a law has no legal effect.

Can a public administration act be challenged?

Yes, through administrative appeal or before an administrative judge (TAR / Council of State) within the legal time limits.
